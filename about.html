About the Workshop

The Dhvanyaloka Workshop

It is believed that the aim of philosophy is to unfold love for knowledge and wisdom. Knowledge, may in itself be considered as an experiential event and as a verbal phenomenon. There are ways and means to directly comprehend an experience and yet another way is to interpret the meaning of a sentential experience. A sentence is a worded experience. Analytical trends in Philosophy though has set the mode of interpreting this form of experience yet there are conceptual frames provided by Indian poetic tradition as well. A poetic sentence differs from a statement of a fact, hence, the condition that makes a sentence poetic in its nature is the prime concern of Kavya-Parampara. Natya and Kavya being two forms of expression try to address the concerned questions and offers a methodology to understand the experience generated out of the two in their own peculiar way. Rasa and Dhvani being the two respective outcomes of Natya and Kavya. The Workshop aims to explore the poetic world through the method of Text-Reading of Dhvanyaloka of Anandavardhan by deliberating on the commentary of Abhinavagupta. The significance of Text-Reading may be viewed in an attempt to trace the impressions that are left by the words on human memory. All words carry their respective meaning by falling back on the memory and with every usage, the meaning of the word discloses within a context duly fulfilling other conditions. The Workshop in its first stage will focus on First Udyot(Chapter) under the able guidance of learned scholars. The first two sessions shall focus on reading and interpreting the text Dhvanyaloka along with commentary. The Third session will be followed by the reflections, queries and comments of the participants. The Fourth session shall be a panel discussion which will address to the dominant concepts(Bhava, Ananda, Sattva, Guna) and theories(Alamkara, Rasa, Riti), Vakrokti, Auchitya) in the Bhartiya Kavya Parmapara.

By Dr. Shivani Sharma
Workshop Director

The Indian Council of Philosophical Research set up by the Ministry of Education, Government of India, was registered as a society in March 1977 under the Societies Act, 1860, but it actually started functioning in July 1981 under the Chairmanship of Professor D.P. Chattopadhyaya.The Council has a broad-based membership comprising distinguished philosophers, Social scientists, representatives of the University Grants Commission, Indian Council of Social Science Research, Indian Council of Historical Research, Indian National Science Academy, the Central Government and the Government of Uttar Pradesh. The Governing Body (GB) and the Research Project Committee (RPC) are the main authorities of the Council.
